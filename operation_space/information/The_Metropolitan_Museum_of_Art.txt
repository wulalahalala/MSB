The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met)
Overview:
The Metropolitan Museum of Art, fondly known as "The Met," is one of the world's largest and finest art museums. Located in New York City, it houses over two million works of art spanning 5,000 years of world culture. From ancient Egyptian artifacts and European paintings by masters like Rembrandt and Van Gogh to American decorative arts and modern photography, its collection is encyclopedic. The museum's mission is to collect, preserve, study, exhibit, and encourage appreciation for outstanding works of art.

Precautions & Tips:

Tickets: It is highly recommended to purchase timed-entry tickets online in advance from the official website to guarantee entry, especially on weekends and holidays.

Security: All visitors must pass through a security check. Large bags and backpacks must be checked.

What to Wear: Comfortable shoes are essential, as the museum is enormous.

Photography: Photography for personal use is permitted in most galleries, but flash, tripods, and selfie sticks are prohibited. Some special exhibitions may not allow photography.

Food and Drink: Several cafés and restaurants are inside the museum. Outside food and drink are not allowed in the galleries.

Plan Your Visit: The museum is too large to see in one day. It's best to pick a few galleries or exhibits you are most interested in and focus on those.
