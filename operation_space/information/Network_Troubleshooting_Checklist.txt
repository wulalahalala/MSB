1. Physical Connections (The Basics)

1.1 Check all cables: Are they securely plugged in (both ends)? Try unplugging and replugging.
1.2 Inspect for damage: Look for frayed or bent cables.
1.3 Check device power: Are the modem, router, and switch powered on? Are lights illuminated normally?
1.4 Restart devices (Power Cycle): Turn off and unplug your modem and router. Wait 30 seconds. Plug in the modem first, wait for it to fully connect (lights are stable), then power on the router.

3. Network Connectivity & Configuration

3.1 Check your IP address: Ensure you have a valid IP address from the router (e.g., typically 192.168.x.x or 10.x.x.x).
3.2 Ping your router: Try to ping your router's internal IP address (e.g., ping 192.168.1.1). This checks the connection within your network.
3.3 Ping an external website: Try to ping a reliable public address (e.g., ping 8.8.8.8 - Google's DNS). If this works but browsing doesn't, it's likely a DNS issue.
3.4 Flush DNS: Clear your DNS cache. In Command Prompt/Terminal, type ipconfig /flushdns (Windows) or sudo dscacheutil -flushcache (Mac).
